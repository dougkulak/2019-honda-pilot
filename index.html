Immediately after purchase, the vehicle displayed a dashboard warning: “Emission System Problem.” This occurred within less than one mile of leaving the dealership, and I documented the warning and odometer at that time.

A pre-purchase inspection performed by an independent shop identified an emissions spacer / “mini-cat” installed on the downstream oxygen sensor. After the sale, the vehicle was diagnosed and the following codes were documented in communications and/or scan reports:

P0159 P0776
  • P0159 — Heated oxygen sensor slow response (Bank 2 Sensor 2) (documented).
  • P0776 — Pressure control solenoid “B” performance / stuck off (documented; transmission-related).
Outcome: After regulatory involvement, the dealer agreed to unwind / rescind the transaction. The lien was paid off and the transaction was unwound. (If you are considering purchase, verify the vehicle’s current emissions compliance and drivability independently.)

Note: This page is not claiming the vehicle is currently unsafe or unlawful to sell. It is a factual record of my experience. Potential buyers should perform their own inspection, confirm smog compliance, and pull vehicle history from trusted sources.
